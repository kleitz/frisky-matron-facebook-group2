{% raw %}THE GAMBLE 85{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He had put together six hundred thousand dollars,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and had then made up his mind that even if he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}never got another cent, he had enough to live upon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}happily for the rest of his existence. A part of his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}previous life had been a very hard one. Now that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he had a chance of luxury, pleasure, and happiness,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}having enough to get everything he wanted, why{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should he go piling up dollars which he could never{% endraw %}
{% raw %}use ? What good were his extra millions to Vander-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bilt ? What benefit were piles of gold to Jay Gould,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pale, gloomy, unhappy, and joyless, on his captain's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}biscuit and glass of cold water ? Over half a million{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all is vanity, so Dunne divided his pile into three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parts and put each portion into a sound investment:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one into a railway, one into a cable, and one into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the English funds. He then started for Paris, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}live happily ever afterwards. He had all the delights{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of extravagance, with the super-added zest of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}security in his own prudence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Yes, sir,' said he, addressing himself during his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}first days in Paris, ' other men have come to grief,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but you cannot. The oil-well of Gilead P Beck{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gave out, but the yield of English consols cannot{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cease. Jabez went smash—he speculated; I don't{% endraw %}
{% raw %}speculate. The landed interest may go to the bad—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I have no cultivated land ; I keep on the mine as an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}additional income. Rich and safe, rich and safe,'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}said he, as he basked in the delights of Paris.{% endraw %}
