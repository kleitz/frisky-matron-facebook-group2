{% raw %}When Yussuff came out of the tent, he strode{% endraw %}
{% raw %}directly towards the place where he had last seen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Miss Browne's carriage. He was, as a rule, ' as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sharp as they make 'em,' but he walked within{% endraw %}
{% raw %}twenty yards of Mrs. Tacker without seeing her.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}That mature beauty had been prepared to welcome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him with effusion, but she saw that he had no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thoughts for her. She felt as if a veil had suddenly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dropped from her eyes, and a cold, heavy feeling, as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}if of a weight of lead, pressed on her heart. She{% endraw %}
{% raw %}turned lividly pale, except two bright red spots{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which burned in her cheeks. She bit her lip until{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it hurt her. The fact is, she was just as much in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}love as she used to be when she was seventeen, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Yussuff was the hero of the hour. He had played a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}man's part among men. He had shown daring.{% endraw %}
