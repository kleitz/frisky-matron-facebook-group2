{% raw %}About this time Mr. Spangam was sitting in his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}private office, and Mr. Spink, the junior partner,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was also in the room warming his back at the fire.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}As befitted a firm of such standing, Mr. Spangam{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was sixty, clean shaven but for gray mutton-chop{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whiskers; his shirt-front, watch charms, and broad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cloth were elderly respectability personified. Spink{% endraw %}
{% raw %}more resembled a festive young stockbroker. A note{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Sir Louis Ventnor lay on the table before them.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Lucky chap, this claimant,' said Spink. ' Three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lives dropped in two months.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' That word claimant is an unpleasant one,' said{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Spangam. ' I hope we shall have no Arthur Orton{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on our hands. We shall have to be very careful.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These papers are very clear, I don't deny; but one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cannot be too particular.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Wasn't there something shady about this young{% endraw %}
{% raw %}man in days of long ago ?' said Spink.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' There was,' said Spangam, ' and a bad business{% endraw %}
{% raw %}too—quite enough to damn a penniless Mr. Ventnor;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in fact, there was talk of his being expelled from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}HeHx, but he left England and vanished, so no action{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was taken. However, that was many years ago,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and there is a statute of limitation for nearly all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}things; besides. Sir Louis Ventnor, with thirty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thousand a year, can afford to forget his chrysalis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stage. By the way, someone told me that he was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seen in London some time ago.'{% endraw %}
