{% raw %}over the Pont Neuf and along the Boulevard St.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}young duke, whose acquaintance he had made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during the polo-match. Everyone staying at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hotel Bossuet is an impecunious duke of the ancien{% endraw %}
{% raw %}regime, or a marquis or a priest. He then strolled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}towards the Hotel Cluny. He knew that place well{% endraw %}

{% raw %}something infinitely restful and withal pathetic in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the garden of Cluny. Outside is the clatter of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Quartier Latin, the tumult of the metropolis, the{% endraw %}

{% raw %}devil. Here all is quiet, aU is calm, all is cool and{% endraw %}

{% raw %}untrimmed by any vandal forester, throw a shade{% endraw %}

{% raw %}steps one reaches cool, calm, quiet rest and peace-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fulness. What would Paris be without Cluny ? And{% endraw %}
