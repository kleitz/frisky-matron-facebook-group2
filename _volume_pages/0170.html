{% raw %}168 A FRISKY MATRON{% endraw %}
{% raw %}her toilette came to more than is spent by the wife{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of many a man who is looked upon as a magnate.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}When Jim took her out on a holiday she paid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nothing, and it never occurred to him that she{% endraw %}
{% raw %}might like to have a little ready money in her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pocket.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The day was lovely. The brightest of sunshine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}showed off every detail of a nice frock. But she{% endraw %}
{% raw %}absolutely had not one single penny. Her forehead{% endraw %}
{% raw %}creased, her lips became set. She thought deeply.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}She went to the window and looked out. Where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was Jim ? She knew all his ways ; she had noted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the care with which he had dressed. A jealous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spasm almost choked her. Then her face became{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hard and bitter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' What is sauce for the goose is sauce for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gander,' said she.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Then she thought again, hesitated for a moment,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}looked at the scrap of paper thrown down by Jim,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and finally sat down with pen, ink, and paper, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commenced to write as follows :{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Sir,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Honesty is the best policy, and I will start{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by letting you know that I am a friend of the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wily detective in London. He has had to-day a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visit from an enemy of yours, who has put him on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to watch you. This enemy of yours is inchned to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}give you a great deal of trouble, and is in as nasty a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}state of mind as any man can be. The detective{% endraw %}
