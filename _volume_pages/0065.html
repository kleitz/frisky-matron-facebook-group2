{% raw %}' Yes,' said Mrs. Tacker, ' the Prince is wonder¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fully civilized, and I believe he will abjure Mahome-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}danism. They are only allowed to drink champagne.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}All other wines are forbidden, but he long since{% endraw %}
{% raw %}emancipated himself from that rule. Some of their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}customs are very peculiar. I remember when I was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Atticalpoor there was an English lawyer, a Mr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jones. He fell very much in love with a young lady,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but he had the disabihty of being already married.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He became a Mussulman, and took the name of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Abdoolah Jones. Well, my dear, Mr. Abdoolah{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jones knew the law, and that a Mussulman can{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have as many wives as he likes, so he married this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}young lady, and lived as a good Mahomedan, with¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}out any danger of a prosecution for bigamy. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}new Mrs. Abdoolah Jones lived very happily for a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time, but a day came when Mr. Abdoolah Jones got{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tired of his new toy. By the Mussulman law, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}husband has but to say to his wife, ' Take your{% endraw %}
{% raw %}best clothes and return to your father's house,' and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}she is thereby divorced. So one fine day the young{% endraw %}
{% raw %}woman found herself with her best clothes back in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}her father's house, and divorced from her Mahome¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dan husband. After this Mr. Abdoolah Jones gave{% endraw %}
{% raw %}up Mahomedanism. He returned to Christianity,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}became again plain Mr. Jones. He and his old wife{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made it up and lived happily ever after, as models of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}connubial bliss.'{% endraw %}
