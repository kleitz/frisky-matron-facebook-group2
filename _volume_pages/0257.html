{% raw %}DON JUAN 255{% endraw %}
{% raw %}CHAPTER XX.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}DON JUAN.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Next day Mrs. Bellairs called on Miss Browne, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found some friends with her. She stayed on until{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the others had left.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Kitty,' said Mrs. Bellairs when they were alone,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' I am so sorry you did not confide in me. I was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altogether in the dark; and, oh dear! oh dear! I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}am afraid I have made an awful mess of every¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thing. Serve me right for meddling; but, my{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dear Kitty, you really might have told me all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about it.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Told you, confided in you about what ? There{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is nothing to confide.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Your engagement, Kitty—your engagement.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' My engagement!' said she with a burning blush.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Nothing is more improbable.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Oh, but I don't mean a mere flirting engagement{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for fun, such as you have been trying for, Kitty;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}though what is fun to you is death for him, poor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fellow ! I mean your real engagement.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' My real engagement! what do you mean ?'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Your engagement to be married.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' I am not engaged to be married.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' I don't mean a flame, pour passer le temps—I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mean your old settled engagement, made originally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by your father, and finally settled in Paris.'{% endraw %}
