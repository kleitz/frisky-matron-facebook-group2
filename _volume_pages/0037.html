{% raw %}among a stream of carriages drawing up before a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}splendid house in the Boulevard des Capucines.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The names of Prince Yussuff and Mr. Dunne passed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from servant to servant, and they made their way{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through a dense crowd to where Mrs. Clement C.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Denning received her guests. Dunne introduced{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Prince, and the lady bestowed upon him fourteen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seconds of her precious time, which should have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been equally divided between five other arrivals.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Yussuff was presently introduced to Mrs. Street-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Houston, a millionairess from Denver City, who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}evidently was under the impression that all princes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are members of reigning families. She had not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been long in Europe. She danced a Lancers with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him, and addressed him very frequently as ' Your{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Royal Highness,' in a voice evidently meant to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}overheard by her friends. She told him her ' At{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Home' day, and introduced him to several other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}members of the ' four hundred,' and in a very short{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time he had invitations enough to keep him fully{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occupied for a fortnight. He had no difficulty in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}overhearing a continuous buzz of remarks about him¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}self. The strange thing was that everyone seemed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to know all about him, though their accounts differed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He was a nephew of the Ameer ; he was the Rajah{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Cochin China ; he was proprietor of Dwalagerri;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he was the son of Gengis Khan.{% endraw %}
