{% raw %}BOHEMIA 31{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The eye of the trained fencer is keen in its com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prehension. Yussuff saw at once that Aramis was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a mere ordinary swordsman. He disarmed him in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a moment.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Yussuff picked up the foil and handed it to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aramis, who gave it to Porthos, saying :{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' I have no chance.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Porthos's vanity was such that he believed himself{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be a good fencer, but he was subdued as easily as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aramis.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Athos then took the foil. Yussuff perceived that he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now had a totally different antagonist to deal with.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Athos recognised the fact that he had to do with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a man good enough to be a maitre-d'armes, but he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had full confidence in himself and played cautiously,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seeking to discover something of his adversary's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style. He learnt all too soon for his peace of mind,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for, after a brisk rally, Yussuff, with the rapidity of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lightning, changed his feet, threw his sword into his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left hand, and taking Athos completely at a disad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vantage, disarmed him.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Monsieur,' said Aramis,' we have long lived with¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}out you ; we have long sought for you, and we shall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mark with a red letter the day on which we have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found you. You are the D'Artagnan of our party.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We will drink to your health. We will raise you on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}our arms. Porthos, lend your strength.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They raised Yussuff aloft, and quaffed fresh{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beakers to his honour, while Estelle, springing on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the table, crowned him with a tureen.{% endraw %}
