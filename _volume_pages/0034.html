{% raw %}32 A FRISKY MATRON{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Then Athos sang a song which he had composed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that morning. It was like a chanson of Alfred de{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Musset, but his friends thought it better.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Estelle followed and afforded a new surprise to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Yussuff. Her whole nature seemed transformed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}She sang a song of the country, a sad song of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}young mother dying in a cottage buried in roses.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}She sang with a tenderness and feeling that produced{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the keen melancholy which is the highest tribute to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sweet melody. W^hen the song ended there was a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}moment of profound silence. Then Estelle's head{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dropped upon her hands, and she burst into tears ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}every eye was moist.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Ah!' cried Aramis, 'her heart is tender; she is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sympathetic; her feelings vibrate in a minor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}key.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'I think,' said Dunne, 'a little "bac" would be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beneficial as a change.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Yes, yes,' said Estelle, 'a little bac;' and she{% endraw %}
{% raw %}immediately became all animation. Cards were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}soon brought. Yussuff knew nothing of the game{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of baccarat, but Dunne explained it to him with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scientific clearness and brevity. Yussuff was an apt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pupil. He seemed to have a natural genius for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}calculation of chances. When daylight came a pile{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of gold lay beside him. The others were cleared{% endraw %}
{% raw %}out; Estelle had lost everything, borrowed and lost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}again.{% endraw %}
