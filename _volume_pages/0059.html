{% raw %}THE GAME 57{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Now, Rip,' said he, speaking as if to a human{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being, ' you must play this game yourself, I know I{% endraw %}
{% raw %}can trust you.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Hussar then mounted, aided by his groom.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By means of his left elbow and his right hand, he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tied a knot in his reins, and dropped them on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pony's neck.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}His groom got a piece of the surgeon's bandage{% endraw %}
{% raw %}linen, and fastened the loop of the reins back to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stirrup-leathers, so that they might not fall over the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pony's head. The Hussar then turned and cantered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to his place. His knees gave all the guidance re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quired.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The French were delighted. Courage always{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appeals to them. ' Comme il est brave ! Bon soldat!{% endraw %}
{% raw %}C'est magnifique!'—until all joined in one grand{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shout, ' Vive le brave I vivent les Anglais !'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Yussuff joined in the acclamations. Instead of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belt, he played in a genuine khummerbund, a piece{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of fine silk, thirty-six feet long, wound eighteen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}times round his waist. He untied a few yards of it,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and wound it on again, tying his left arm to his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}body. He then, by means of his right hand and left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}elbow, tied his reins in a loop, dropped them on his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pony's neck, got them hitched to his stirrup-leathers,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and cantered to the Chasseur's place, requesting him{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to relieve Texas, and Texas to keep goal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Generosity, equally with courage, will always{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appeal to the French nature. Tumultuous applause{% endraw %}
{% raw %}followed, and in countless carriages ladies stood{% endraw %}
