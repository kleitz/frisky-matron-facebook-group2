{% raw %}CRIME 211{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jim got the job, and was completely successful.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He spent a fortnight in Paris. He was delighted to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}find that almost at once he found himself at home.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He required no map, except the one he carried in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his brain. With this and his compass he made his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way about. By the end of that fortnight he knew{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paris as well as he knew London. He was de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lighted with the study of its beauty, and of its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crimes. There is a glamour about the City of Light{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which gripped his fancy. One day he sat in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cafe Rococo. He had read of the Eiffel mystery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The sight of the Tower brought all the details to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his mind, and he thought of it a good deal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jim felt very elate. From Paris he wrote several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}times to Jenny; he had left her in charge of all his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}business in London. She opened his letters, and,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as her handwriting was almost indistinguishable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from his, she very often answered them too. Jim{% endraw %}
{% raw %}knew his work could not be in safer hands. He{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wrote her very long letters, for Jim had a fluent pen,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and he bragged of his cleverness, and gave her full{% endraw %}
{% raw %}details of the crimes on whose traces he came.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jennie lived a very pleasant life during his absence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}She had lots of pocket-money, thanks to a certain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gentleman of high position to whom she supplied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}information, and the Count de Villers-Saxel escorted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}her to several good plays.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The day she received Jim's letter from the Cafe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rococo was that immediately preceding the date on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which should arrive the monthly allowance paid for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}14—2{% endraw %}
