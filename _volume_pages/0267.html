{% raw %}DON JUAN 265{% endraw %}
{% raw %}* Who is the lady, Mrs. Bellairs ? I challenge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}you to tell me the name.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Mrs. Tacker, so there------'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Mrs. Tacker! 'Pon my honnaw------'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Oh, don't attempt to deceive me. Why, she{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boasts that you are her slave.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Aw, deah me ! The poor thing has mistaken my{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sentiments ; I am so sorry.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' You like her much better than you do me.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' No, most certainly not. I love—like you im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mensely more.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' But she says when you go to call on her you put{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on your best pair of boots, and when you come to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}see me you only put on your second best.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' This is most serious,' said he, deeply annoyed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' I always have twelve pair, and they are all the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' And you tell her things you never tell me. You{% endraw %}
{% raw %}confide in her, you exchange secrets with her. Oh,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mr. Simkin, Mr. Simkin !' and she raised her hand¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kerchief to her eyes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Weally, this is too bad of her ; I never told her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}any secwets.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' You told her all about Mr. Caldwyn's gambling,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and how he was going to be sold up, and is to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}retrieve his circumstances by a rich marriage.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' This is most extwordinary. I never spoke a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}single word about Mr. Caldwyn to her in my life.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Besides, he hasn't touched a card for two years, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he is not going to be sold up.'{% endraw %}
