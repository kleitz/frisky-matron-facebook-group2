{% raw %}began to think that Miss Browne would have liked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him better if he had been rather a scamp. He re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}membered that ' Every woman is at heart a rake.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A really good man may be liked by a girl, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}always with a little leaven of contempt, unless he be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a curate. He felt that Miss Browne would have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liked him better if he had stifl a little of his former{% endraw %}
{% raw %}devilment in his character, and wild ideas of getting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into a row at the Empire, or bolting with Mrs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tacker, passed through his heated brain. He{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thought how exquisite it would be to rush back and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be forgiven. But such ideas were merely the aber¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rations of a moment. The fact is, he had fallen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in love with Miss Browne before he had spoken{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to her, in a stupid blundering masculine sort of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way, and to him the idea of any other woman was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}repugnant. He had had a past, but that was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dead and buried, and at present he lived for Miss{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Browne, and for her only.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}And what were her views ? It would be difficult{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to say. She intended ultimately to get married ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}she thought it would be nicer than living alone.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}And she began to like Caldwyn. She never asked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}herself if she were beginning to love him, but her{% endraw %}
{% raw %}feelings were something in the same state as a solu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion on the point of precipitation. A shock might{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lead to a rapid crystallization, and that crystal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}might be a jewel from the treasure of the great god{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Amor, who carries many arrows in his quiver, but{% endraw %}
