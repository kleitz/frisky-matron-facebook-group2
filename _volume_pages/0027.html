{% raw %}' We will join you with pleasure,' said Athos ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' but for the other ladies—that will keep for some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other time. We would be too many. We are now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}six—quite enough for a room at the Trois Soeurs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Those rooms on the second story are not large. Do{% endraw %}
{% raw %}you yet know your Paris ? If not, I may mention{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that the Trois Sceurs lies close by, and is a house in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which one may trust. Also, monsieur, you and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Estelle, and our good friend Hail Columbia, will{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dine with the three mousquetaires on Tuesday at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Verey's.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A very little time saw the party seated at table in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a small but comfortable room at the Trois Soeurs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Estelle was in a state of ecstasy. She was a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sparkling, handsome girl, one of those beautiful{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Southerners whom destiny so often leads to Paris.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Her profile was perfect. Masses of the darkest silky{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wavy hair, eyebrows and eyelashes almost black, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perfect complexion, and eyes which sparkled or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}softened, but were always beautiful, entranced the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beholder. Her figure was sumptuous, and with that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}artistic genius which every Frenchwoman possesses,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}directly she obtained money she had learned how{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to dress to perfection. She had been successful, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of late she had lived more by rule; but she was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dehghted at her return to the Boulevard St. Michel.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}fools; they know dry old books, but they do not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}know what one feels—what one believes. There is{% endraw %}
