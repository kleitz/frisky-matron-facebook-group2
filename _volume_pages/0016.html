{% raw %}14 A FRISKY MATRON{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gunnis Sing was not so well educated as Yussuff,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who wrote to him accordingly.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Having paid his bill and tipped the waiter in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}princely fashion, Yussuff returned to the Gare de{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lyon, where he found his three servants sitting on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their heels, with their backs against the wall, waiting,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}calm, content and patient. The Kitmeghar Mhad-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hurah sprang to his feet, put on his turban, slid off{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his slippers, and made a low salaam,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}' Now,' said Yussuff, who spoke English to all who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}knew it, ' get your baggage from the cloak-room.'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mhadhurah soon obtained a bundle, some six feet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in circumference, which contained as much as could{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be packed in two ordinary portmanteaus. It was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wrapped in a sheet of coloured Bombay linen of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great strength, and tied up with knots whose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}number and intricacy would have puzzled anyone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but a native to the manner born,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A fiacre quickly conveyed master and man to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gare St. Lazare. The time wanted ten minutes to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the starting of the boat train.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}hours you will be in London, and see your wife.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}How long is it since she left you ?'{% endraw %}
